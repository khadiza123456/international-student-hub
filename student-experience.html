Essential Survival Tips

Practical advice from experienced international students

1

Build Your Network Early

Connect with other international students and local students during orientation week. Join clubs and societies that match your interests.

2

Master Time Management

Balance academic commitments with exploration and social life. Use digital tools to schedule study sessions and leisure activities.

3

Understand Local Laws

Familiarize yourself with visa regulations, work restrictions, and local laws to avoid legal issues during your stay.

4

Embrace Cultural Differences

Be open to new customs and traditions. Don't be afraid to ask questions when you're unsure about social norms.

5

Stay Connected Back Home

Schedule regular calls with family and friends back home, but also focus on building your new life abroad.

6

Utilize University Resources

Most universities offer free counseling, academic support, and career services specifically for international students.
